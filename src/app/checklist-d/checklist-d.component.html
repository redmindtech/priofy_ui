9.Procedure Checklist-D
Step Action (Hazard/Precaution if applicable) Yes/No NOTE: Outside OT to make sure Decoke header is properly drained before lining up decoke air to the furnace. Utilize procedure “202-02-R-01 Furnace F-22_0, Empty Decoke Drain Header”. AICR Action: EU-2021Nov17-117483l. 1. Has IOT's confirm that “arch temperature” warm up ramp set point is 0.83 ºC/min (equivalent to 50 ºC/hr or to increase 200 ºC/hr in 4 hours) and that all fuel controllers are properly enabled and in the correct mode/mode attribute.
Note: in the Warm Up Arch step the furnace temperature increase is controlled by the arch temperature. 2. OOT to open decoke air ONIS blind and 6” main B/V 3. COOT to open 4” decoke air B/V downstream each control valve FV-22X0-10A/B. 4. Note: IOT to inform Utility before start decoke airflow.
IOT to enable Decoke Air controllers and start flow through the coils at the default set point of 3.0 Mt/h per pass (side) by resetting HV-22X0-07 through its HS. 5. OOT to light manual burners as requested by IOT (to maintain fuel gas pressure between 0.2 to 0.4 kg/cm2g) and by following Furnace, Burner Light off procedure. Note 1: fuel gas pressure needs to be maintained as low as possible to keep the flame close to the tile when steam enters the firebox, in order to allow the scanner to see the flame. Note 2: burner light off sequence attached in Appendix A must be followed. Note 3: “add burners” alarm set point is 0.5 kg/cm2g (stops the ramp) and “cut burners” alarm is 0.2 kg/cm2g. Low-pressure constraint controller set point is 0.15 kg/cm2g. 6. When 10 burners are lit the air registers of the lit burners should now be opened to 3 notches. 7. When around 20 burners are lit all the lit burners air registers should be opened out to 100%. 8. IOT to request OOT to close the 3” steam drum start up vents when the steam drum pressure is 5 kg/cm2g. 9. IOT to ensure air is flowing through each coil by: *Checking that all COTs are rising at about the same rate. *Checking that all Venturi Ratios are about the same value.
Note: The accuracy of the venturi ratios will be poor while the feed header pressure is low, and the coil airflow is low. 10. IOT to clear pluggage from suspect coils by increasing the air flow (max. 3.6 Mt/h) on that side of the furnace. 11. OOT to start warm up of the MS steam header by draining condensate upstream the 8” B/V to pad.
Keep ¾” bleed cracked open until the line is warm (dry steam coming from the drain). 12. OOT to drain spool piece between 8” B/V and HV-22X0-06 to pad and then line up steam trap to MC header.
Keep ¾” bleed cracked open until the line is warm (dry steam coming from the drain). 13. OOT to open MS EBV, HV-22X0-06. 14. Once condensate has been drained OOT to open MS steam 8” B/V. 15. IOT to make sure that both steam control valves FV-22X0-22A/B remain closed. 16. OOT to warm up the steam lines up to steam control valves FV-22X0-22A/B. *Keep ¾” bleed upstream each FV and ¾” bleed upstream 10” spectable blind cracked open until the lines are warm (dry steam coming from the drain). 17. HOOT to drain the Dilution Steam to FPH Outlet lines (Pass 1&2) by opening the ¾” bleed downstream each FV.
Keep ¾” bleed cracked open until the lines are warm (dry steam coming from the drain). 18. OOT to open 2” steam B/V to ethane feed line and drain condensate by opening the ¾” bleed to pad upstream of HV-22X0-02.
Keep ¾” bleed cracked open until the lines are warm (dry steam coming from the drain). 19. OOT to open 3/4” bleed downstream ethane feed HV-22X0-86 to pad.
Keep ¾” bleed cracked open until the line is warm (dry steam coming from the drain). 20. OOT to crack open 10” B/V downstream each ethane feed control valve FV-22X0-14A/B and drain the ethane line to FPH backwards by opening the ¾” bleed downstream the FV to pad. Keep ¾” bleeds cracked open until the line is warm (dry steam coming from the drain).
When all the condensate is drained out close the drains and the 10” block valves. 21. Open the upstream and downstream 4” B/V of FV-22X0-08 and start closing the 2” bypass then take the control using the control valve FV-22X0-08. Phosphate and Morpholine injection to main BFW line to be started. Dosing rate to be decided based on blowdown sample.
Note: Separate injection point valves for old F-2280 line should be kept closed. 22. Before starting the steam flow through the coils the combustion air flow to the furnace FC-22X2-15D-16D should be increased to 60 M t/h to dilute the effect of steam on the scanners. IOT to enter manual set point. 23. IOT to reduce the draft to (-8) mmH2O by entering a manual draft set point, before starting the steam flow through the coils. 24. The IOT should put dilution steam FV-22X0-22A/B on program Auto then enable the steam flow via the HMI when the arch temperature is >350°C and start steam flow VERY SLOWLY and in MANUAL, by following the next sub-steps
FV-22X0-22 A/B will ramp steam flow on control vale output. *Request the OOT to visually check the firebox to see if it starts to look foggy due to steam. *Monitor the steam flow reading to each side (FT-22X0-22A/B), the HTC-1 outside temperature on each side (TT-22X0-17A/B) and the radiant inlet temperature (TT-22X0-19A/B) and pressure (PT-22X0-18A/B) on each side to identify if steam is flowing or not yet.
Note 1: Continue increasing Arch temperature @ 50C/hr.
Note 2: the steam flow control will automatically enable at 400º C if the IOT has failed to enable. It is believed that the current set up of starting the steam flow through the coils at 3 M t/h could affect the scanner readings and trip the furnace on “loss of flame”. 25. OOT to line up steam lines purges (DS and steam to FPH) to C-2317 once all the steam lines are confirmed dry. Make sure that the 2” B/V at the common header is open. 26. IOT to ensure steam is flowing through each coil by: *Checking that all COTs are rising at about the same rate. *Checking that all venture ratios are about the same value.
Note: the accuracy of the ratios will be poor while the feed header pressure is low, and the coil steam flow is low. 27. IOT to clear pluggage from suspect coils by slowly increasing the steam flow on that side of the furnace. 28. OOT to line up LS “purge steam” to: *Secondary TLE inlet PT *Tertiary TLE outlet PTs. *PT between both CG MOVs. *Both CG MOVs and Decoke MOV seats (HV-22X0-13A and HV-22X0-14 and Cavity). Make sure the inlet header 2” B/V are open and steam traps operational. 29. IOT to ensure that SHP steam flow is exiting the vent silencer. 30. IOT to continuously monitor the fuel gas pressure and OOT to continue lighting off more burners as required.
Note: FG pressure should be maintained between 0.2 -0.4 kg/cm2g. 31. OOT to continuously monitor the firebox to see if it starts to look foggy due to steam entering it. 32. When the steam flow is >10 T/H/coil the decoke air flow is backed off by closing the FV’S in auto.
Note: the steam flow is expected to reach its full flow at an arch temperature of 650ºC. 33. OOT to close decoke air ONIS blind and 6” main B/V once there is no more air flow to coils. 34. OOT to close 4” decoke air B/V downstream each control valve FV-22X0-10A/B. 35. IOT to disable Decoke Air controllers and confirm that the ONIS “Close DI” is true. 36. When Decoke air HV-22X0-07 is closed HV-22X0-02 (purge steam to ethane feed line) should open 25% on program logic. 37. OOT to fully open downstream block valves of the ethane feed control valves FV-22X0-14A/B and metallic car seal provided. 38. IOT to confirm that ethane feed control valves FV-22X0-14A/B come fully open in auto to allow starting steam flow through the feed preheat coils. 39. OOT to line up steam drum and sTLE continuous blowdown to header. When steam drum header pressure reads > 12 kg/cm2. 40. If needed, adjust the steam drum blow down to maintain the BFW economizer coil outlet temperature below 312ºC.
Note:this is done by increasing intermittent blowdown at the steam drum so there is more flow through the ECO to prevent high temps. 41. IOT to ensure Furnace sequence auto-move to Warm up COT HSSB step occurs. Warm up continues at 50°C per hour.
Note: warm up switches from Arch control to COT control when both sides average COT temperature is higher than 450°C and dilution steam flow on each side is higher than 5 Mt/h.
