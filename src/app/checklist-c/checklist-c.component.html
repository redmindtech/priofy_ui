8.Procedure Checklist #C - Light Off
Step Action (Hazard/Precaution if applicable) Action 1. Has IOT manually moved the BM sequence to Stand By step and also manually moved the Furnace control sequence moves to Purge & Light Off step.
Note: In this step the burner management sequence performs pressure test, purge and light off of burners. This Purge & Light Off step is used for “cold” light off. 2. After one purge volume is completed has the OOT pressed the field reset button, so that IOT can move the BM sequence to Fuel Header Purge step. If the pressure test and hold pressure (holding time = 30 seconds) is performed in this step prior to the pad/depad. Then ,does pad/depad to atmosphere is done 6 times in “auto”. If purge permissive are not maintained or pressure up & holding do not meet the requirements, furnace will trip.
Note: this Step will only be when the furnace fuel gas header was pressure tested by N2, to get rid of N2 from the fuel gas header. This provides for proper fuel gas composition for Light Off. 3. IOT's ensure that whether BM sequence returns back to Standby step after Fuel Header Purge step is done.
Note: all other purge permissive (Purge airflow) must be satisfied during this operation. The purge counter re-starts once the sequence returns to the Standby step. 4. OOT check and make sure that the furnace to be lit and the adjacent furnaces area is cleared of all personnel, including the Furnace OOT.
Note: All platform levels must be checked prior to confirm that the furnace area (included the adjacent furnaces area) is clear. 5. After one purge volume is completed, Has IOT requested OOT to push local reset button (field flashing green light should come on solid).
6. Has IOT moved BM sequence to Pressure Test step within 15 minutes of field reset.
Note: in this step the fuel gas system is tested for leaks or open manual burners. 7. Has IOT observed pressure test of fuel gas system. Pressure Up: bypass DB&B opens, local PCV opens and header is pressurized to 0.2 kg/cm2g within 100 seconds, or trip occurs.
Hold: bypass DB&B closes, pressure must not drop more than 0.02 kg/cm2 (10%) or trip occurs.
Note: purge permissive must be maintained all the time, if not furnace will trip. 8. If Pressure Test fails, has OOT de-pressured fuel gas headers to radiant box.
9. When Pressure Test is complete BM sequence moves automatically to Firebox Purge step.
Note: this step is to ensure all flammables are purged from firebox prior to light off. Purge permissive must be maintained all the time, if not furnace will trip. 10. Has the OOT ensured that all floor burners air registers are fully open, except the 4 automated burners that must be closed.
11. Has OOT ensured that all wall burners air registers are closed.
12. After 5 purge volumes are completed, does the BM sequence moves automatically to Purge Complete step.
Note: in this step purge permissive are monitored and must not be violated. 13. Has IOT ensured that ALL parameters below are OK so he can initiate the light off. *Steam drum level.
*Firebox draft.
*All automated burners are ready for light off.
*purge permissive true.
*No combustibles in firebox.
*Fuel control valves closed.
Note: these are really part of the purge permissive that the Triconex is checking. 14. Has IOT moved BM sequence to Light off Burners step within 15 minutes of Purge complete otherwise the furnace will go to Total Trip.
15. Has IOT manually RESET the BM & BNR alarms (unlatch). The remote burner light off cycle is automatically initiated in this step. Auto burner Light off attempt sequence is #7, #30, #21 and #16.
Notes:
  • Pressure regulators installed around the control valves control the burner fuel gas pressure at 0.2 kg/cm2g during light off.
  • Only one burner will be attempted at a time. Each burner is automatically attempted once and it is only attempted if its own sequence is in the Process Wait step, and no alarms on this burner.
  • At least one burner in each cell must be successfully lit at the end of the light off sequence, or the furnace will move to the Total Trip step (based on flameout Total Trip).
  • Burners with invalid flame detected are not sequenced for light off attempt. At least one purge volume (time) must elapse between unsuccessful light off attempts if the furnace is below auto ignition.
  • The time the burner is allowed to have its B/V open attempting light off is limited to 10 seconds.
  • If a burner fails to ignite (flame scanner does not indicate flame) the burner B/V is closed & the igniter is retracted and de-energized.
  • One purge volume must occur before subsequent attempt to light the next burner.
16. Do Ensure that atleast one burner in each zone must be lit to move the Burner Management sequence to the Run step.
17. Has OOT adjusted the air damper for the automated burners which are lit to one notch for steady flame.
Note: don’t want to have operator normally on the furnace during initial light off. 18. Has OOT ensured Igniters are not stuck inside the firebox..
Note: ensure Igniter panel is reset in substation. 19. Has IOT confirmed BNR sequence moves after light off attempt:
  • Unsuccessful light, sequence moves to total Trip step (HV closes).
  • Successful light, sequence can be moved to Run step by IOT.
20. If the Light off of the furnace failed, has OOT manually depressurized the burner headers.
21. Has IOT manually move BM sequence to Run step if successfully achieved at least one burner per zone.
Note: IOT must move the BM sequence as soon as he gets permission to do so. If a burner gets an invalid flame (say mismatch) that alarm trips as long as in Light off step 22. Has IOT confirmed that Furnace sequence has auto moved to Warm Up Arch step.
23. Has Auto burners that did not successfully light during the auto-light cycle, have it been re-attempted via the HMI.
